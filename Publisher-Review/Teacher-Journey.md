# Teacher Journey Review

## What works

- `/teachers/` contains classroom-specific proof, teacher testimonials, STEM/SEL framing, and two email-capture opportunities.
- The site has a meaningful Teacher Resources category and relevant classroom/read-aloud articles.
- The language understands Grades 1–3 and the practical needs of teachers.

## Critical friction

- The primary navigation sends teachers to `/teachers-guide/`, not the canonical `/teachers/` resource page.
- `/teachers-guide/` does not redirect. It has no H1, no working guide form, and its only detected in-content action is an email link.
- `/teachers/` has six H1 elements and duplicate IDs for its repeated email fields.
- Three teacher-resource PDF links return 404, including the prominent free Mariana Trench guide.
- No public Explorer Passport destination was discovered.
- The teacher journey is fragmented among `/teachers/`, `/teachers-guide/`, category archives, and individual posts without a single dependable resource index.

## Classroom adoption opportunities

- Make `/teachers/` the canonical entry and point navigation there.
- Present downloads by book, grade, time required, standards/skills, and file type.
- Add clear expectations around email delivery, privacy, and immediate download behavior.
- Surface sample pages, learning objectives, classroom time, and teacher testimonials near each download CTA.
- Connect each product page back to the matching educator resources.

## Recommendation

NO-GO for educator outreach until routing, duplicate form IDs, and broken downloads are corrected and tested end to end.
