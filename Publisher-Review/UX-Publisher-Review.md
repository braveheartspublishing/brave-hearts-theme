# UX and Publisher Review

## Executive view

The visual voice is warm, adventurous, and distinct. Home communicates “Big Places. Brave Hearts.” effectively, and the combination of books, real-world exploration, and teacher material supports the intended Adventure–Education–Character promise. The experience breaks down when visitors act: several discovery paths fail, core pages lack clear hierarchy, and trust infrastructure is incomplete.

## Major findings

- Home has the clearest message and strongest visual hierarchy, but five of six Learning Hub paths are broken. That turns the most educational section into a trust-eroding dead end.
- Books lacks an H1 and no intentional in-content CTA was detected. Visitors can see books, but the path from interest to purchase is weak compared with Shop.
- Shop presents all six products with working product and add-to-cart links, but it lacks an H1 and is not the main navigation destination for “Books.”
- About lacks an H1. Its personal origin story is valuable, but the page needs a clearer first-screen publisher promise and stronger proof of educational credibility.
- Contact lacks an H1, a form, and a clear in-content CTA. It reads more like a learning-resource showcase than a reliable contact destination.
- Teachers is content-rich and persuasive, with teacher testimonials and classroom positioning, but six H1 elements, duplicated forms, and split routing undermine professionalism.
- Mailchimp Test and Sample Page are publicly published and should not be present at theme freeze.
- Thirteen posts remain in Uncategorized, weakening browse clarity and editorial authority.
- Privacy Policy, Terms, and Explorer Passport were not found.

## Messaging and brand

- Adventure is consistently visible through destinations, covers, and language.
- Education is strongest on Teachers and in the blog, but broken downloads and Learning Hub routes undercut the promise.
- Character is present in “Brave Hearts” and kindness/courage references, but it is less explicit in purchase and archive journeys.
- “Nature is the greatest classroom on Earth” is supported conceptually, but not consistently reinforced as a recognizable publisher proposition across core pages.

## Visual and responsive review

- All 88 URLs rendered at 1440 px and 390 px without capture failure.
- Mobile pages generally reflow, but very long editorial pages and large original images produce heavy vertical journeys.
- Footer H4 headings create repeated hierarchy jumps across templates.
- External Squarespace-hosted imagery creates inconsistent loading behavior and weakens long-term maintainability.

## Priority order

1. Repair broken journeys and downloads.
2. Consolidate Teachers routing and remove public test/sample pages.
3. Fix core-page H1 and heading hierarchy.
4. Add legal/trust pages and a dependable Contact flow.
5. Optimize images and complete Lighthouse/keyboard/contrast testing.
