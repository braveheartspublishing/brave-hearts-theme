# Parent Journey Review

## Journey

Landing → discovering books → trust building → learning resources → shopping → checkout

## What works

- Home quickly establishes a memorable adventure-book proposition.
- Featured paperback products link to real product pages.
- Shop lists all six editions and exposes direct add-to-cart actions.
- Product pages provide conventional WooCommerce purchase controls.
- The editorial library offers substantial help around reading levels, reluctant readers, read-alouds, STEM, and age-specific recommendations.

## Friction points

- Home’s educational promise is damaged by five broken Learning Hub cards.
- The prominent “Shop the Books” journey commonly routes through Books, yet Books has no detected purchase CTAs and no H1. Shop is a separate, stronger transaction page.
- Contact does not provide a clear form or in-content contact action.
- Privacy Policy and Terms were not found, reducing trust before account creation or checkout.
- Public Sample Page and Mailchimp Test content make the staging site feel unfinished.
- Checkout could not be reviewed in a populated state without mutating the session. Empty checkout redirects to Cart.
- Large external images can slow discovery, especially on mobile connections.

## Recommendation

Do not freeze until a parent can move from Home to a book, understand why it is educational, reach Shop, and verify legal/contact trust without encountering a dead end.
