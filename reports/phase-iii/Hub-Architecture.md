# Explorer Expedition Guides — Final Hub Architecture

## Published now

| Hub | Posts | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Explorer Expedition Guides | 35 | Main curated entry point; combines all supported paths without duplicating posts. |
| Reading & Growing | 22 | Deepest collection; reading transitions, levels, bridge books, reluctant readers, and family guidance. |
| Science & Geography | 7 | Combines two individually thin subjects into one useful real-world learning collection. |
| Educator Resources | 4 | Sufficient focused material for read-alouds, classroom guides, and school relationships. |
| Book & Brand Stories | 2 | Small but distinct trust collection supported by substantive origin and review articles. |
| The Mariana Trench | 5 direct posts plus book connections | Strongest destination collection. |
| Mount Everest | 1 direct post plus book connection | Published as a compact destination path, not a standalone thin page. |
| Family Resources | 29 secondary relationships | Integrated audience path using Reading & Growing and Science & Geography content. |

All collections are anchored sections of the existing `/teachers/` page. This preserves a known working URL and avoids publishing thin standalone pages or requiring database-created pages.

Existing published supporting pages retained without URL changes: `/bridge-books/`, `/reading-levels/`, `/reluctant-readers/`, `/mariana-trench/`, and `/read-alouds/`. They remain available for their current search intent while the new guide landing page provides the unified curated route.

## Combined

- Science + Geography: seven posts collectively support a useful hub; separate pages would be thin.
- Family Resources: audience layer rather than another category or duplicate content collection.
- Book One/Two/Three collections: represented through destination and book connections rather than duplicate hub pages.
